Getting Started

If you are just getting in to Poker, I recommend playing the smallest stakes you can find. .01-.02 cent has been invaluable to my poker journey by allowing me the financial freedom to get in as many hands as I want. When you first start, it is highly unlikely you will be profitable for atleast a year, and to mitigate this, play as tiny as possible!

Cash Game Bankroll

There are varying opinions on how many buy-ins you need to play a stake of cash. For me, personally, I find value in being able to shrug of any swings, so I recommend never playing a cash game without 40+ buyins. This will allow you to cope with variance and you should never go broke with a good winnrate.

Tournament Bankroll

Tournaments have higher variance than cash games, and therefore you need more buy-ins. I have personally felt the swings of tournament poker, and it is soul crushing. In order to remain sain, I recommend at least having 200+ buy-ins to play tournaments.

Discipline

I firmly believe the most important attribute of a serious poker player is discipline. This will keep you from spewing buy-in after buy-in out of tilt, keep your bankroll tightly managed, and allow you to play comfortably. Without structure and discipline, bankrolls that took months to build can be crumbled in days.

Setting Goals and Challenges

In order to stay motivated, keep track of, and continually improve, I recommend setting challenges and goals for yourself. This will structure you bankroll and force you to improve to move up in stakes. A good target winnrate for Cashgames is 7+ BB/100 hands, and for tournaments anything over 10% ROI is outstanding.
